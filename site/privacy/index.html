What data we collect, and why

When you donate

Donations are processed by Stripe, our payment provider. When you donate, Stripe collects your name, email address, billing address, and payment details (such as your card or bank account information). We receive a record of your donation — your name, email, amount, and the date — but we never see or store your full card or bank details; those stay with Stripe.

We use this information to process your donation, send you a receipt, keep accurate financial records, and (where appropriate) thank you and keep you updated about the impact of your support.

Legal basis: performing our agreement with you to process your donation; complying with our legal obligations to keep financial records; and our legitimate interest in administering and acknowledging donations.

When you join or get involved

If you fill in our "get involved" form, it is hosted by Tally. We collect the information you choose to give us — typically your name, email address, location, and how you'd like to help — so that we can coordinate volunteers and local groups and keep you informed about activities.

Legal basis: your consent, and our legitimate interest in organising and growing the movement.

When you contact us

If you email us, we keep your message and contact details so we can respond and, where relevant, follow up.

Legal basis: our legitimate interest in responding to enquiries.

When you browse this website

We use Google Analytics to understand how people use our site so we can improve it. This sets cookies that collect information such as the pages you visit, roughly where you are (by country/region), and the type of device and browser you use. Analytics cookies are only set after you accept them via our cookie banner. See "Cookies" below.

Legal basis: your consent.

Who we share your data with

We do not sell your personal data. We share it only with the service providers that help us operate, each acting on our behalf or under their own privacy terms:

  • Stripe — payment processing. Stripe's privacy policy.
  • Tally — hosting our sign-up form. Tally's privacy policy.
  • Google Analytics — website usage analytics. Google's privacy policy.
  • GitHub Pages — website hosting. GitHub's privacy statement.

Some of these providers are based outside the UK, so your data may be transferred internationally. Where it is, that transfer is protected by appropriate safeguards (such as UK "adequacy" regulations or standard contractual clauses / the UK International Data Transfer Agreement).

How long we keep your data

  • Donation and financial records: at least 6 years, as required by UK tax and company law.
  • Volunteer / sign-up information: for as long as you remain involved or interested, and deleted on request.
  • Correspondence: for as long as needed to deal with your enquiry and any follow-up.
  • Analytics data: retained in line with Google Analytics' data-retention settings.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • have inaccurate data corrected;
  • have your data erased in certain circumstances;
  • restrict or object to how we use your data;
  • request a copy of your data in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent at any time (where we rely on consent); and
  • complain to the regulator.

To exercise any of these rights, email uk@pauseai.info. You also have the right to complain to the UK's data protection regulator, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), at ico.org.uk.

Cookies

Cookies are small files stored on your device. We use:

  • Analytics cookies (Google Analytics) — only set after you accept them. They help us understand how the site is used.
  • A preference cookie — to remember your cookie choice so we don't ask every time.

When you donate, Stripe's secure checkout sets its own cookies, which are necessary for processing payments and are governed by Stripe's privacy policy. You can change your analytics choice at any time using the "Cookie settings" link in the page footer, or by clearing cookies in your browser.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we'll change the "last updated" date at the top. If the changes are significant, we'll make that clear.
